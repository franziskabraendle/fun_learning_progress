You can start the experiment now.






In the following study, you will be presented with a series of different grids, where each tile on the grid is an option you can choose to sample.

Different tiles show different amounts of points. The points are based on the location of the tile on the grid, as tiles in the same area tend to yield similar point values. The tiles are color coded as a visual aid with darker colors corresponding to larger point values.

Your task consists of exploring grids for 10 minutes. You can explore as many grids as you like and you can spend as much time as you want on each grid. When you don’t like to explore a grid any further you can click on “Next” to move on to the next grid. But you need to reveal at least five tiles of a grid before you can move on to the next one. You can click on every tile only once.

After the 10 minutes have run out, clicking on “Next” will lead you to a questionnaire.

Try to explore the grid now by clicking on different tiles. When you’re ready to start, click on “Next”.
